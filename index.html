The Beer Mile is a super fun event allowing us to bring together three great things! Beer, Running and Fundraising!

What is a Beer Mile?

When the race director says "Go" you will drink one 12 ounce beer then run a quarter mile, then repeat the process three times. This results in the consumption of four beers and the running of four quarter miles (hence the beer mile).

The Rules

  1. Each competitor drinks four cans of beer and runs four laps (start - beer, then lap, then beer, then lap, then beer, then lap - finish).
  2. Beer must be consumed before the lap is begun, within the transition area which is the 10 meter zone before the start/finish line.
  3. Competitors who vomit before they finish the race must complete one penalty lap at the end of the race (immediately after the completion of their 4th lap).
    Note: Vomitting more than once during the race still requires only one penalty lap at the end.
  4. Each participant must define a designated sober driver, or reserve a car through a taxi/limo provider, or use public transportation to get home from the event. Safety first people!

FAQ

  • Do I bring my own beer?

    • No, the beer is provided! Generously dontated! We will thank our sponors on race day!

  • Oh my! Any tips?

    • Yes, try to belch after each beer, get that gas out!

  • What if I throw up?

    • No worries, it happens to the best of us. Simply wipe your chin and run an extra lap! - The good news is, your name will be added to the Bucket Award! - Congrats!

  • Can I bring a child to this event?

    • Yes you can. We will have space at the end of the great lawn where kids and parents can hang out. Children can not be in areas where beer is being served. Keep that in mind.

  • Who can participate?

    • Anyone over 21 can participate in the race. All shapes and sizes welcome, but nobody under 21 will be allowed into the grounds. You will need 21+ ID to be granted access to the event.

  • Packet Pickup?

    • Pick up your race packet prior to the event, 5:30pm - 6:30pm.

  • Why are you doing this?

    • This is a fundraising event supporting the Byron Community Foundation.

  • What if I do not want to run, but just watch as a spectator?

    • Spectators who do not want to run the race or drink are welcome to come and enjoy the festivities. We simply ask that you make a $10 donation to Byron Community Foundation when you arrive at the event.

  • Can I wear a costume?

    • NEW IN 2015! There are awards for best costumes! Get dressed for fun and win!
